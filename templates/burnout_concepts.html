Basic Concepts

Understanding Multiple Linear Regression applied to Student Mental Health & Burnout.

What is Student Burnout?

Student burnout is a state of chronic physical and emotional exhaustion caused by prolonged academic stress. It affects motivation, performance, and overall mental well-being. Key contributing factors include high stress levels, anxiety, poor sleep, and excessive study hours without adequate rest.

In this model: We predict the burnout_score (0–10) of a student based on 4 measurable inputs: stress level, anxiety score, sleep hours, and study hours per day.
Multiple Linear Regression

Unlike Simple Linear Regression (one predictor), Multiple Linear Regression uses two or more independent variables to predict an outcome. This gives a much more realistic and accurate model.

y = b₀ + b₁x₁ + b₂x₂ + b₃x₃ + b₄x₄
  • y burnout_score — the value we predict (0–10)
  • x₁ stress_level — overall stress (0–10)
  • x₂ anxiety_score — anxiety intensity (0–10)
  • x₃ sleep_hours — hours of sleep per night
  • x₄ study_hours_per_day — daily study duration
  • b₀ Intercept — baseline burnout when all inputs are 0
How the Model Learns
1
Dataset
1,000,000 synthetic records of students. We sample 20,000 for fast training without losing accuracy.
2
Train / Test Split
80% of data trains the model. 20% is used to evaluate its accuracy on unseen records.
3
Fitting the coefficients
The algorithm finds the best b₀…b₄ values that minimize the Mean Squared Error (MSE) across all samples.
✓
Prediction
Input the 4 values → the model computes the formula and returns an estimated burnout score.

How We Measure Model Quality

R² Score (Coefficient of Determination)

Measures how much of the variance in burnout_score is explained by the model. A value of 0.85 means the model explains 85% of the variation. Closer to 1.0 is better.

MSE (Mean Squared Error)

Average of the squared differences between predicted and actual values. A lower MSE means the model's predictions are closer to reality. Closer to 0 is better.
